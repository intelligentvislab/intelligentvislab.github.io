I am an Associate Professor in the School of Information Technology at York University, where I direct the Intelligent Visualization Lab, funded by the Canada Foundation for Innovation (CFI) and the Ontario Research Fund (ORF). I am a member of the Graduate Faculty for both the Department of Electrical Engineering & Computer Science and the School of Information Technology. Before joining York University I was a postdoc in the HCI group at Stanford University, where I worked with Maneesh Agrawala.

My research unites Natural Language Processing and Information Visualization to open new frontiers in language-based data interaction. My work has produced widely adopted benchmarks and models — including ChartQA, Chart-to-Text, UniChart, and ChartGemma — that have been adopted by leading AI organizations such as OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic. I have published nearly 100 papers in premier venues across AI, visualization, and human–computer interaction, including ACL, EMNLP, IEEE VIS, CHI, and UIST.

**Research interests:** Information Visualization, Natural Language Processing, Human–AI Interaction, and Multimodal AI.
